# Positioning territory — Markdown template

Each of the three Phase-2 territories is one Markdown file in this exact shape. Dense and structured, not a prose essay. Fill every section from the research + the four frameworks. Three territories must be **genuinely distinct** (different word, enemy, category, beachhead) — not three taglines for one idea.

```markdown
# <Product> — Positioning <A|B|C>: "<Territory name>"

**Generated:** <date if provided> · **Status:** Strategy proposal — option <A|B|C> of 3.
**Frameworks:** Ries & Trout · Dunford · Blue Ocean · Baker.
**Axis of this option:** <the single dimension this territory leads on — e.g. output quality / breadth / headcount / the moat>.
**One line:** *<the position in one sentence>.*

---

## 1 · The position
<One short paragraph: the frame and why it fits the product + the evidence.>
**Hero sentence:** *<the position as a single customer-facing sentence>.*

## 2 · Who it's for
<The beachhead + a sharp named persona (the who that feels the pain most and buys fastest). Say who it is NOT for.>

## 3 · Dunford's six components
1. **Competitive alternatives:** <what they do today without you>
2. **Unique attributes:** <what you have that alternatives don't — prefer shipped, hard-to-copy>
3. **Value (+ proof):** <the benefit they care about, and the proof>
4. **Target-market characteristics:** <who has those values acutely>
5. **Market category:** <the frame; anchor + re-segment>
6. **Trend:** <the wave it rides, if any — must reinforce a durable attribute>
```
