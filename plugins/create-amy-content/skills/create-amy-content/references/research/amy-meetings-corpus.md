# Amy Benson - Meetings Corpus (spoken sales/discovery register)

Distilled 2026-07-09 from ~50 Gemini meeting transcripts (2025-03 to 2026-05) in the private customer-feedback archive: discovery calls, demos, adviser catch-ups, ecosystem meetings and student outreach across six segments. Roughly **150,000 words of Amy's own spoken turns** - by an order of magnitude the largest evidence base in this package, and the only one showing her selling live.

**How to use this file:** it extends `amy-spoken-voice.md` with the live sales/discovery register. Rules derived from it are marked `[Source: meetings corpus]` in the voice files; consult this file when a spoken-register judgement is close. Quotes are redacted (external people → roles, companies → descriptors, prices/deal terms dropped) and cited by segment + date only; full provenance stays in the private archive.

## The transcript caveat (binding)

These are machine voice transcriptions. Turn boundaries shred mid-sentence, words are misheard ("Dialogue/Dialog" for Diolog, "ASICH" for ASIC, "Westpak", "Fortnite" for fortnight), one file mislabels another speaker's turns as Amy's, and all punctuation is the transcriber's guess. Therefore this corpus is **never evidence for punctuation, spelling, or sentence mechanics** - that layer rests on her written corpus alone. It IS strong evidence for: lexicon, question shapes, ask shapes, objection handling, warmth beats, product framing aloud, and call structure - anything confirmed across multiple independent calls.

## Lexicon hierarchy (live)

Measured on Amy turns; the written-corpus hedges are NOT the spoken ones.

- **Dominant (every call, high density):** "you know" (439× in one segment alone - spoken filler, never written), "kind of" (181×+), "I guess" (25-57× per segment), "really", "I think".
- **Sincerity markers (recurring, cross-segment):** "to be honest", "to be very transparent", "genuinely", "honestly".
- **Simplifier:** "very simply / very very simply". **Doubled intensifiers generally:** "really really", "very very". **"super"** as intensifier (US calls).
- **Affirmation cluster:** "that makes sense", "100%", "exactly", "of course", "definitely".
- **Transition/close tokens:** "all righty", "I'm conscious of time", "I could talk about this for ages, so I'll cut it there".
- **Demoted:** "I'd say" (3× in 35k words) and "I find" (1×) are RARE live - they belong to the interview register, not sales calls. Weight accordingly.
- **Aussie idiom, live status:** "keen" confirmed (sparse); "heaps", "touchwood", "footy", "how good" confirmed; **"arvo" and "brains trust" never observed** in ~150k words - treat as chat-register only.
- **Motto status:** "you don't get what you don't ask for" - never once spoken across the corpus. Its live cousins: "you don't know what you don't know", "people love to help - there's no shame, just reach out", "you need the expertise to know what to ask for". The motto is interview/keynote evidence, not a call line.
- **Retired phrases: CLEAN.** No bricks/Rome, no "I came up through" anywhere (she says "I come from an IR background" - keep guarding the boundary between that and the retired phrasing).
