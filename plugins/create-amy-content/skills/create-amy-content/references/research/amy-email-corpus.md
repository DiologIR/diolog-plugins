# Amy Benson - Email Voice Corpus (redacted)

Verbatim emails Amy sent from her diolog.com.au address over ~12 months, grouped by tone. Signatures, quoted replies and auto-generated text were stripped at source; recipient names and non-Diolog company names have been redacted here (`[…]` role tags) - the words are otherwise Amy's exactly. **These samples are style ground truth, never fact sources**: the deals, dates, prices, and people inside them must never migrate into new drafts.

## Voice summary (from the source corpus)

- **Openers:** greeting + a warm human beat - "Hope you had a great long weekend!", "Great to catch up and chat last week." A genuine, specific compliment when reaching out cold.
- **Register:** professional but conversational; contractions throughout; Australian spelling and idiom ("arvo", "long weekend", "floats your boat", "the brains trust").
- **Sentence style:** clear and unfussy. Longer, well-organised sentences for proposals; short and breezy for casual notes. Uses spaced hyphens " - " to append thoughts (never an em dash).
- **Structure:** for anything substantive she recaps context, lays out numbered/bulleted options (bold label, plain prose after), and anticipates questions.
- **Calls to action:** soft and optional - "Worth a quick look?", "Let me know your thoughts!", "Happy to find another slot if none of these land."
- **Attitude:** generous, low-pressure, service-oriented; repeatedly makes things easy for the other person. Non-defensive about rejection; actively invites candid feedback.
- **Sign-offs:** "Regards, Amy", "Best,", "Thanks, Amy", "Cheers," by formality.
- **Product framing (recurring phrasing):** "the workspace for everything investor-facing", "governance-first AI for investor communications", "checked against what you've already disclosed", "without disclosure risk or mixed messages".
