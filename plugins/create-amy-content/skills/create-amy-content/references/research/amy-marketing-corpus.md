## Observed marketing mechanics

- **Benefit beats are concrete and time-anchored:** "in an afternoon, not a week", "Hours back, every week", "before your coffee's cold", "Onboard a client in under an hour" - outcomes a buyer can picture, never adjectives.
- **Headlines trade on a vivid specific or a sharp contrast:** "Less time in the inbox. More time on the story." / "Scaling the advice, not the headcount." / "Catch the contradiction before the market does." Two short balanced beats, or one concrete image.
- **The "no X, no Y, no Z" relief list:** "No prompt-writing, no copy-paste, no hunting for the latest numbers" / "no switchboard, no generic inbox" - names the annoyances removed.
- **Short declarative fragments as section beats:** "Proactive, not reactive." "Onboard. Switch. Repeat." "Specific, not spin." "Disclosure, without doubt."
- **The fear is named calmly, then answered:** "Catch the contradiction before the market does", "No model trained on Client A ever sees Client B" - risk copy is precise, never alarmist.
- **Zero hype adjectives** (no revolutionary/seamless/cutting-edge anywhere), zero exclamation marks in body copy.
- **Plain-english test:** her stated bar - simple enough for a LinkedIn message, understandable without translation. The consumer register (for-investors) drops further into second person and even plainer words ("the companies you own").
- **CTAs are two words, action-first, low pressure:** "Book a demo", "Explore the platform", "Get the app".
- **Category naming is calm and infrastructural:** "Investor communication infrastructure", "governance-grade", "governed workspace" - authority through precision, not superlatives.
- **Per-audience register shifts on the same product truths:** IR page sells time-back and story, CoSec page sells control and review confidence, advisory page sells scale and separation, investor page sells directness - the underlying claims stay consistent (a live example of positioning per audience).
