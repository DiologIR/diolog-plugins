## Interview register (structured spoken - panels, podcasts, pitches)

Verbatim excerpts from a founder-series interview:

> "Startups are all about ruthless prioritization, even more so when you're a solo founder."

> "I really, really stand by the fact that you don't get what you don't ask for. And I think in sales, people are afraid to ask kind of like awkward questions maybe when they're talking to potential customers, like, 'How much would you expect this to cost?' or 'Does this seem reasonable?' or 'Is there anything holding you back?'"

> "So actually, there's a whole learning piece before you get to the pitch, I find."

> "Every person makes the company. The company is the people."

> "I, as a person, I'd like to describe myself as quite frank and quite honest. And like with the sales thing, you know, I was asking like pretty, pretty hard questions, I'd say. You know, questions like, 'What's going to happen if we disagree on something?' … Or 'What's your attitude towards, you know, fixing a bug at 12:00 a.m. at night?'"

> "We all know the saying, 'Rome wasn't built in a day,' but the second part of that saying goes something like, 'but a brick was laid every hour.' So I imagine every day, whether it be a good one or a bad one, I'm still laying a brick. I'm still building Rome."

> "They said back to me, 'Oh, so you mean you're just ruthlessly prioritizing every day?' And having it said back to me, I was like, 'Oh yeah, like that's what I actually really need to do.'"

Observed habits in this register:

- **Answers begin "Yeah," / "Yeah, of course." / "Yeah, definitely."** then build.
- **Hedged self-positioning:** "I guess", "I'd say", "I find", "to an extent" - she qualifies while still landing a firm view.
- **Frameworks named plainly:** value-versus-effort scale, ruthless prioritisation - she reaches for a named method, then illustrates it with her own case.
- **Signature lines she returns to:** "you don't get what you don't ask for"; the Rome/bricks saying; "the company is the people".
- **Stories carry the point:** the insight arrives inside an anecdote with reported dialogue ("they said back to me…"), not as an abstract claim.
- **Direct questions as a tool:** she demonstrates her method by quoting the exact awkward questions she asks.

## Casual conversational register (chat with friends/team)

Observed habits (from transcribed casual conversation; excerpts withheld as personal):

- High-energy acknowledgements: "Oh my god", "that's so funny", "so cute", "what the heck", "that's crazy".
- Rapid agreement runs: "Yeah, yeah", "Oh yeah, yeah", "Mm-hmm".
